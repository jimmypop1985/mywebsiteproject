Describe the role of IT in modern business

“organizations are increasingly turning IT into a driving force in all aspects of business.” [forbes.com] [1]

To describe IT’s role in modern business we must first understand what IT is. Outside of the professional environment, we manage our lives through phones, communicate through apps, pay for things online, or speak to friends on the other side of the planet. Simply put, IT is a way of making life easier, a way of making life better.

Modern businesses now use IT as the driving force for their business, IT departments are experiencing tremendous changes as their roles expand to impact customer service, sales, and even business strategies.[1 ] As someone that has run small companies I truly understand the impact that useful IT solutions can have in all aspects of a business, from marketing and communication to compliance and admin. IT is there to create solutions for problems, it is a service that should be designed to make its customers' lives easier. It doesn’t make businesses successful, people do. IT’s role is a more subtle, humble one and it grows in influence every day.

Having the ability to code alone isn’t enough to complete a digital project, you need some core methods, skills, and tools that help ensure an efficiently completed product. Project management is a key part of the process. All tasks must be broken down into manageable pieces and then delegated to the appropriate members of the team. There are a lot of project management methodologies out there, each with their strengths and weaknesses.

One such methodology is agile, where teams will develop the software in iterations that contain mini increments of the new functionality. This allows software to be released in increments. Agile methods can, however, be labour intensive and require real-time communication between users. There are several different types of agile methods such as scrum, crystal, extreme programming (XP), and feature-driven development (FDD). Agile methods can be used in conjunction with others as hybrids.

The most traditional methodology is the waterfall method, which follows a rigid, linear process consisting of rigid, sequential phases Each phase must be complete before the next phase can be started. It is an easy approach to understand and manage. It can be slow and costly due to its rigid nature.[2]

Describe the risks that impact digital projects - What causes digital projects to fail? Why?

In project management, risk is the likelihood of something not going to plan [5]. Here we will explore some of the situations that could go wrong in a project and established situations to mitigate them. During a project some factors are outside of your control, the market is one of them, for example, if you are designing a new piece of software there is a chance that a competitor could be doing the same thing. This is common in any business and there is nothing you can do to stop it. It is possible, though, to work around it if you have assessed that risk, for example, you could target a different corner of the market, or change your plan to get the software out earlier to get an advantage over the competition. Internally many scenarios could occur, for example, you are working on an ambitious project, but it is a startup and the client suddenly reallocates the budget to another department leaving you with fewer resources than originally planned for. In this case, you would recognise the risk early on and have a plan B to mitigate this risk.

[10]
A good digital project planning process will have the “Known/Known” risks built-in as well as the Known/Unknown. The Known/Known plan will involve coming up with a Plan B along with the contingency plan for resources such as time allocation, budget, and your team. A good planning process will also address the “Known/Unknown” risks with the idea of preventing them, and if that is not possible, coming up with a Plan B to mitigate. There will always be risks in a project that will fall into the bottom two quadrants, this is part and parcel of the project management reality. However, if you can identify them or learn to understand them through thorough risk assessment they will move into the top quadrant of the matrix and can be mitigated. Risk assessment is about being thorough, critical, and realistic to come up with a plan for any eventuality during a project. [5]

Describe how you would go about selecting IT systems and software - how do you select the right kind of technology for the job.

The main factors that will affect the IT systems and software you will use will be budget and the specific requirements of the job. If the job doesn’t require high-end graphics or processes that require high-speed processing, it may be unnecessary to purchase top of the range MacBook pros for your team. Conversely, a cheaper laptop might not be able to do the jobs you require efficiently. You may wish to use the free version of some software to allow some budget for some paid software. A start-up might have a very small budget so it may be necessary to hustle a bit and use cheaper resources whereas an established company doing animation might need a fleet of expensive hardware and premium animation studio software to complete the job. The role of the project manager is to sensibly and creatively manage the resources at hand and make the best decision for it.

Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects - How does GDPR impact digital projects?

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is an European law covering privacy and security. If an organisation collects or uses data from people in the EU, it is required to adhere to GDPR. Breaches of this law can result in hefty fines for a company. GDPR regulates how companies can use people’s personal data and gives those companies accountability for what they do with it. It outlines the measures that must be taken to secure people’s information, regulations on how people consent to how their data is used and when you are allowed to process someone's data.[7]GDPR is there to give people who put their data online the confidence that it is being used appropriately and legally. There have been some much publicised cases of companies using people's data without their express permission, creating the need for this law. In the real world companies must have transparency on how they will use an individuals data and explicitly ask for permission to use it. [11]
